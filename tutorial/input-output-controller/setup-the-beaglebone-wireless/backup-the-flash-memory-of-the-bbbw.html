Backup the flash memory of the BBBW

  • Power on the BBBW

    With the BBBW powered off insert a blank micro SD card into the BBBW. The micro SD card slot is near the USB port

    Insert a micro-USB cable into the BBBW and connect the other end into your linux computer. Make sure the micro-USB cable supports data transfer. You can buy plenty of cheap cables that only supply 5V power. Throw those away!

    Wait for the BBBW to boot - it takes about three minutes. You should see the wireless (WL) LED, the BlueTooth (BT) LED light up and the USB0 LED blink.

  • Connect to the BBBW

    Open a terminal and log into the BBBW with

    ssh root@192.168.7.2

    In the initial configuration there is no password required for root

  • Backup the flash memory 
    /opt/scripts/tools/eMMC/beaglebone-black-make-microSD-flasher-from-eMMC.sh

    It will take about 20-30 minutes to backup the flash. The terminal will give you progress on the backup. When the backup is complete, the BBBW will power down itself.

    Eject the micro-SD card and insert into your linux computer (not the BBBW). Edit the /boot/uEnv.txt file on the Linux partition on the microSD card

    Insert '#' in the beginning of the line with cmdline=init=/opt/scripts/tools/eMMC/init-eMMC-flasher-v3.sh

    Remove the '#' on the line with cmdline=init=/opt/scripts/tools/eMMC/init-eMMC-flasher-v3-bbbw.sh

  • To restore the flash memory,

    Insert the backup SD card into the BBBW and power on.The backup will begin automatically. It will take 15 minutes to restore. During the restore only the USBn LED scan back and forth and the WL and BT LEDs are off. When the restore is complete, the BBBW powers down
