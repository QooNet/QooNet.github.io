Backup the flash memory of the BBBW

  • With the BBBW powered off insert a blank micro SD card into the BBBW. The micro SD card slot is near the USB port
  • Insert a micro-USB cable into the BBBW and connect the other end into your linux computer.
    • Make sure the micro-USB cable supports data transfer. You can buy plenty of cheap cables that only supply 5V power. Throw those away!
  • Wait for the BBBW to boot - it takes about three minutes.
    • You should see the wireless (WL) LED, the BlueTooth (BT) LED light up and the USB0 LED blink.
  • Open a terminal and log into the BBBW with
    • $ ssh root@192.168.7.2
      • In the initial configuration there is no password required for root
    • Backup the flash memory by typing:
      • $ /opt/scripts/tools/eMMC/beaglebone-black-make-microSD-flasher-from-eMMC.sh
      • It will take about 20-30 minutes to backup the flash. The terminal will give you progress on the backup.
      • When the backup is complete, the BBBW will power down itself.
      • Eject the micro-SD card and insert into your linux computer (not the BBBW).
        • edit the /boot/uEnv.txt file on the Linux partition on the microSD card
          • add the '#' on the line with
            • 'cmdline=init=/opt/scripts/tools/eMMC/init-eMMC-flasher-v3.sh'.
          • remove the '#' on the line with
            • cmdline=init=/opt/scripts/tools/eMMC/init-eMMC-flasher-v3-bbbw.sh
        • To restore the flash memory, insert the backup SD card into the BBBW and power on.
          • The backup will begin automatically.
          • It will take 15 minutes to restore.
          • During the restore only the USBn LED scan back and forth and the WL and BT LEDs are off.
          • When the restore is complete, the BBBW powers down
